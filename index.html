History

Formula 1 is a high-technology motorsport (car racing) that hosts an annual world championship, very popular since 1950. This championship originated from the Grand Prix competition, which took place in Europe before World War II. Formula 1 (F1) is regulated by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), headquartered in Paris.

Great race car drivers are part of Formula 1's history. Among them are Ayrton Senna, Michael Schumacher, Alain Prost, Nelson Piquet, Juan Manuel Fangio, Niki Lauda, Rubens Barrichello, Lewis Hamilton, and many other renowned champions over the years.

Currently, each race is called the Formula 1 Grand Prix, followed by the name of the country where it takes place. The races happen on racetracks and some street circuits in Europe, Asia, Oceania, and the Americas. The most famous street circuit is the Monaco Grand Prix in Monte Carlo. The scoring system, which has changed over the years, now rewards up to the 10th place in each grand prix. The results of each race are converted into points that, when added, define two champions each year: drivers and constructors.
